Grammar · Day 2
Appositives: Spot Them
✏️
Teacher reviews appositives and models identification
📋 Teacher Recording Notes
• Day 2 = identify (Grammar SWYK day)
• Quick recap: an appositive renames the noun right before it, set off by commas
• Model 1–2 examples before the game
• Preview the SWYK
• ~2–3 minutes

An appositive renames or describes the noun right before it. It is set off with commas on both sides.

Isaac, a ten-year-old Choctaw boy, lived in Mississippi.

Appositive: “a ten-year-old Choctaw boy” — it renames Isaac and adds detail, set off by commas.

✅ Virtue Watch: JUSTICE
A virtue Isaac shows this week

Justice means working to right what is wrong — even when it is costly, complicated, and not guaranteed to succeed.

🌿 Virtue in action — JUSTICE:

Isaac as a ghost reviews everything that has happened: the fire, the Trail, Naomi’s captivity, the rescue. He cannot undo any of it. But he can bear witness — he can remember accurately. Memory is a form of justice.

📚 What Good Readers Do
Reading lens for today

Good readers notice when the oppressed use the land itself as a tool. In Chapter 26, the rattlesnake appears. As you read: who controls it? How is it used? And what does its appearance say about the relationship between the Choctaw people and the natural world?

💭 Hold this as you read.
📖 RWM — Read • Notice • Try
“That must be why ghosts know everything. While everybody else is sleeping, we are watching.”

✏️ As a Writer
Isaac as narrator shares a ghost insight directly with the reader — breaking the fourth wall slightly. The humor is gentle but the observation is real: ghosts have time.
