Sentence 2 — Fix It

This sentence has mistakes. Rewrite it correctly in your notebook, using the model as a guide.

johnny once the best apprentice in boston now wandered the streets looking for any work he could find

Corrected:

Johnny, once the best apprentice in Boston, now wandered the streets looking for any work he could find.

Compare with the model: “Johnny” is a proper name—capital. “Boston” is a proper noun—capital. “Once the best apprentice in Boston” is an appositive renaming Johnny—commas before and after it. Add a period at the end.

🌱 Morphology: Roots dict & scrib/script

🌱
Morphology · Apply
Roots in Context: Chapter 3
🌱
Teacher applies dict and scrib/script roots to Chapter 3 vocabulary

Two Latin roots appear throughout academic and literary vocabulary. Dict means “say” or “declare.” Scrib and script mean “write.” Knowing these roots helps you decode unfamiliar words.

📌 From Chapter 3

describe (de + scrib) — Johnny had to describe his bad hand to every master in Boston

verdict (ver + dict) — every shop seemed to deliver the same verdict: you won’t do

predict (pre + dict) — no one could predict that Johnny would remember the Lyte cup

manuscript (manu + script) — at the Observer, Rab handled manuscripts and printed broadsides

prescription (pre + script) — the unspoken prescription for his despair was to go to Merchant Lyte

RWM — Read like a Reader, Read like a Writer, Mimic
“He sat a long time with his arms hugging his knees. Now he knew what to do.”
📖 Read like a Reader
Johnny has just spent weeks failing, being rejected, and being told there is no work for him. Then he breaks down crying at his mother’s grave. Why does he feel so certain in this moment? What does the calm after despair tell you?
✏️ Read like a Writer
Two short sentences—almost like two heartbeats. The first shows stillness; the second shows clarity. Notice how the author uses short, simple sentences at turning points. Why not a longer sentence here?
🖊️ Mimic
Write two short sentences that show a character reaching a decision after a long struggle. First sentence = the pause. Second sentence = the resolution.
He/She waited. Now ___.

Read: Chapter 3
An Earth of Brass

Mr. Hancock commissions a silver sugar basin from the Lapham shop—the biggest order they’ve had in years. Johnny takes charge of the work, pushing Dove and Dusty hard. He visits Paul Revere, who critiques his design and offers to buy his time. Mr. Lapham warns Johnny about his pride. When the silver must be poured on Sunday, Dove hands Johnny a cracked crucible out of spite. The molten silver spills—Johnny’s hand is horribly burned, his thumb fused to his palm. His career as a silversmith is over.

🎯 Today’s focus: Learn what EVIDENCE is—the specific proof from the text that supports a reason.

Reason

Evidence from Chapter 3

Notice: Evidence is specific. It names what happens, where, and what it shows—not just a vague summary.

📋 Today’s Tool: The Proof Test (3 checks)

✅ From the text — Did it actually happen in the book?
✅ Connects to my reason — Does it directly support what I said?
✅ Specific — Does it name something concrete, not just say “he shows this”?

My reason: ___
My evidence: In Chapter ___, the author shows this when ___.
