Sentence 2 — Fix It

This sentence has mistakes. Rewrite it correctly in your notebook, using the model as a guide.

the people who watched from the wharfs were mostly woman and child who kept very quiet so the british would not notice

Corrected:

The people who watched from the wharves were mostly women and children who kept very quiet so the British would not notice.

Compare with the model: “Wharfs”→“wharves” (irregular plural of wharf). “Woman”→“women” and “child”→“children” are irregular plurals. “British” is a proper adjective—capital. “The people” starts the sentence—capital T.

Morphology · Explain & Justify
Roots Across the Novel: Chapters 1–4
🌱
Teacher connects dict and scrib/script roots to words from across Chapters 1–4

Two Latin roots appear throughout academic and literary vocabulary. Dict means “say” or “declare.” Scrib and script mean “write.” Knowing these roots helps you decode unfamiliar words.

📌 Explain the Prefix

You have now seen con-/com- in three chapters of Johnny Tremain. This is your chance to show you understand why the prefix changes form.

Why “com-” before b, m, or p? These three sounds are all made with your lips. When you say “com-” before “b” or “m,” the sounds flow together naturally. “Con-” before “b” would be awkward to say.

Examples: combine, command, compress — all before labial (lip) sounds.

connect, conflict, convene — before all other consonants.

From Chapter 8: When General Gage moved to combat colonial resistance, the Sons of Liberty needed a congress of their own—a meeting to decide what to contain and what to let spread.

🌱

Practice: Prefix/Root Match! Tap each word, then tap its meaning.

combat
to fight against; to struggle with (com- + bat: to beat together)
command
to order; to lead (com- + mand: to put in charge together)
congress
a coming together to meet and decide (con- + gress: to walk together)
contain
to hold together; to keep within bounds (con- + tain: to hold)
compel
to force someone; to drive together (com- + pel: to drive)
🌱 All matched! You can explain con-/com-.

Argument Writing — Day 4: The Conclusion
✓ What Is a Claim? → ✓ Reasons → ✓ Evidence → ★ Putting It Together → ○ Draft → ○ Revise & Publish

🎯 Today’s focus: Learn how to write a strong CONCLUSION—one that echoes your claim, acknowledges what you’ve proven, and leaves the reader with something worth thinking about.

👀 Watch How — Alex

Alex now has all the pieces and puts them into an Argument Map. Watch how the claim, reasons, and evidence connect.

📌 Alex’s Claim

Johnny Tremain is changed by his suffering—not destroyed by it—because he learns that he needs other people.

Reason 1

Because Rab helps him when no one else does.

Evidence 1

In Chapter 4, Rab arrives at the jail with food and blankets, and finds a lawyer to defend Johnny at trial.

Reason 2

Because Cilla and Isannah’s loyalty saves him.

Evidence 2

Cilla testifies at trial about seeing the cup in July, which proves Johnny owned it before the alleged theft.

Notice: Each reason answers “why” the claim is true. Each piece of evidence names something specific that actually happened.
