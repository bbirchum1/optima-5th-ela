This Week's Fluency Passage
As they came out from the shelter of the trees and the Great Meadows stretched before them, Kit caught her breath. She had not expected anything like this. From that first moment, in a way she could never explain, the Meadows claimed her and made her their own. As far as she could see they stretched on either side, a great level sea of green, broken here and there by a solitary graceful elm. Was it the fields of sugar cane they brought to mind, or the endless reach of the ocean to meet the sky? Or was it simply the sense of freedom and space and light that spoke to her of home?
— Elizabeth George Speare, The Witch of Blackbird Pond, Chapter 8

🗺️ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video above. Listen to how your teacher reads each word clearly, keeps a smooth pace, and uses expression to show Kit's wonder and longing as she sees the Meadows for the first time.
2
Read for Accuracy
Read the passage aloud slowly. Focus on saying every word correctly — especially solitary, graceful, and instinctively. If you stumble, go back and try that sentence again.
3
Read for Smoothness
Read the passage again. This time, keep your voice flowing. Don't stop between words — let the sentences move like a river, pausing only at commas and periods.
4
Read for Expression
Read it one more time. Now bring Kit to life! Let your voice show her awe and wonder at seeing the wide-open Meadows. Slow down at the questions near the end — let the reader hear Kit thinking, searching for why this place feels like home.
5
Record and Submit
When you feel ready, record yourself reading the passage with your best accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Upload your recording to Canvas.
✅ Fluency Self-Check
🎯 Did I say each word correctly?
🌊 Did my reading sound smooth — like flowing water, not choppy waves?
🎭 Did my voice show the feeling — Kit's wonder, her longing for home, her sense of freedom?
