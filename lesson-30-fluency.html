📜 Week 30 Final Fluency Passage — Chapter 18: The Proposal
With sudden decision, Praiseworthy whipped off his hat. His mouth was as dry as a cured salmon, but he kept talking.

“If there’s going to be any proposals of marriage I intend to be the first in line. I’ve got no vices to speak of, although I have taken to smoking strong cigars. To say it right out, Miss Arabella — will you do me the honor of becoming my wife?”

Constance and Sarah began jumping up and down on their toes.

Aunt Arabella was smiling through a wet sparkle in her eyes. “Marry you? Why, of course. I thought you’d never ask.”

“By the Great Horn Spoon!” Praiseworthy gasped.
— Sid Fleischman, By the Great Horn Spoon!, Chapter 18

🥇 Your Final Fluency Path
1
Listen
Praiseworthy is nervous for the first and only time in the novel — “his mouth was as dry as a cured salmon.” The speech should feel halting, building, earnest. Then “By the Great Horn Spoon!” — his shock that it worked. Listen for how your teacher paces the nervousness vs. the relief.
2
Read for Accuracy
Focus on: vices, Arabella (Ar-uh-BEL-uh), and the full formal phrase “will you do me the honor of becoming my wife.” Don’t rush the proposal — it took 18 chapters to get here.
3
Read for Smoothness
The passage moves between action, interior thought, and dialogue. Let the movement feel natural: whip off the hat (action), dry mouth (interior), then the speech (dialogue), then the girls jumping (action), then Arabella’s answer (dialogue), then his shock (one line). Each beat needs its own moment.
4
Read for Expression
Praiseworthy: formal, nervous, speeding up as he commits. Arabella: warm, smiling, slightly amused that it took this long. The girls: delighted, physical. And then Praiseworthy’s “By the Great Horn Spoon!” — the novel’s title, said by its most dignified character, at the moment he is completely, perfectly flustered. Give it everything.
