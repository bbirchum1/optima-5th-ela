🕊️ Week 4 · Fluency

This week you read about New England's blazing autumn, the night the Connecticut Charter disappeared, and Nat locked in the stocks — while Kit wrestled with questions about love, loyalty, and what it really means to be free. Today you will practice reading a passage from Chapter 14 with accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Great readers don't just say the words — they bring the story to life with their voice.

This Week's Fluency Passage
She stood in the doorway of her uncle's house and held her breath with wonder. The maple tree in front of the doorstep burned like a gigantic red torch. The oaks along the roadway glowed yellow and bronze. The fields stretched like a carpet of jewels, emerald and topaz and garnet. Everywhere she walked the color shouted and sang around her. The dried brown leaves crackled beneath her feet and gave off a delicious smoky fragrance. No one had ever told her about autumn in New England.
— Elizabeth George Speare, The Witch of Blackbird Pond, Chapter 14

🗺️ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video above. Listen to how your teacher reads each word clearly, keeps a smooth pace, and uses expression to show Kit's wonder and delight as she sees New England autumn for the very first time.
2
Read for Accuracy
Read the passage aloud slowly. Focus on saying every word correctly — especially gigantic, emerald, topaz, garnet, and fragrance. If you stumble on a word, go back and try that sentence again.
3
Read for Smoothness
Read the passage again. This time, keep your voice flowing — don't stop between words. Let the sentences move forward like a river, pausing only at commas and periods.
4
Read for Expression
Read it one more time. Now bring the scene to life! Let your voice rise with excitement at "the color shouted and sang around her." Slow down at the final sentence — "No one had ever told her about autumn in New England" — and let the listener feel how surprising and beautiful this moment is for Kit.
5
Record and Submit
When you feel ready, record yourself reading the passage with your best accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Upload your recording to Canvas.
✅ Fluency Self-Check
🎯 Did I say each word correctly?
🌊 Did my reading sound smooth — like flowing water, not choppy waves?
🎭 Did my voice show the feeling — Kit's wonder and delight at seeing New England autumn for the first time?
