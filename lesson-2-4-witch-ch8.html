Morphology · Suffixes
Suffixes -ion and -ation
🌱
Teacher introduces -ion and -ation suffixes

This week you learned that the suffixes -ion and -ation turn verbs into nouns. They mean “the act or process of.” When you add these suffixes, the spelling of the base word sometimes changes.

📌 Key Concept: -ion / -ation Examples

react → reaction (the act of reacting)

condemn → condemnation (the act of condemning)

educate → education (the process of educating)

recommend → recommendation (the act of recommending)

🧩

Grammar · Explain
Why Sentence Combining Matters
✏️
Teacher explains why writers combine sentences

All week you have been working with sentence combining. Good writers combine short, choppy sentences into longer, smoother ones by using compound subjects and compound predicates.

👉 From the novel

Choppy: “Kit pulled onions. Judith pulled onions. They weeded the garden.” — These three sentences are short and repetitive.

Combined: “Kit and Judith pulled onions and weeded the garden all morning.” — One smooth sentence with a compound subject (Kit and Judith) and a compound predicate (pulled… and weeded).

Combining sentences makes writing flow. It also shows readers how ideas connect.

🎯

Practice: Pick the Combined Sentence! Tap the sentence that uses combining well.

Kit pulled onions all morning. Kit and Judith pulled onions and weeded the garden all morning.
Mercy smiled and told Kit about the dame school. Mercy smiled. Mercy told Kit.
Hannah lives alone. Hannah lives alone by Blackbird Pond and keeps only cats for company.

Spelling · Challenge
Suffix Spelling Changes — Can You Get Them Right?
🔠
Teacher introduces the spelling challenge

When you add a suffix to a word, the spelling of the base word sometimes changes. You might drop a letter, double a letter, or change a letter. Try each one below.

recommend + ation
Does the base word change when you add -ation?
recommendation
No change to the base word — just add -ation directly.
begin + ing
What happens to the final consonant?
beginning
Double the final n before adding -ing (short vowel + single consonant rule).
happy + ness
What happens to the y?
happiness
Change the y to i before adding -ness.
