Today’s Focus
What makes a comparison essay do more than just describe two things?

A true comparison argues why the difference matters. It doesn’t just say “Novel 1 does X and Novel 2 does Y.” It explains what that difference reveals about the theme, the author’s choices, or the human experience. The comparison paragraph is where your analysis lives.

Corrected: The most significant difference between the two novels is not the definition of courage itself but the context in which it must be exercised. Both authors suggest, however, that courage at its core is a choice made under pressure — one in which the character could have chosen safety.

Writing Workshop: The Comparison Paragraph
The paragraph that makes your essay a real argument
📌 How to Write the Comparison Paragraph

This paragraph is the heart of your essay. It does four things:

1. State the similarity: Both novels agree that... [what they share about the theme]

2. State the key difference: However, [Novel 1] shows this through [method], while [Novel 2] reveals it through [different method].

3. Explain WHY the difference matters: This difference is significant because... / What this reveals is...

4. Connect to the bigger idea: Together, these two novels suggest that [theme insight].

💡

Example comparison paragraph (about courage): Both Kit Tyler and Praiseworthy demonstrate that courage does not require drama. However, Kit’s courage is inward — she endures suspicion and isolation without public announcement, while Praiseworthy’s courage is quietly outward — each calm action performed in full view of others who notice exactly what he is doing. This difference is significant because it shows two distinct social contexts: in Puritan New England, courage had to be invisible to be safe; in Gold Rush California, courage could be visible because no one had the authority to punish it. Together, these two novels suggest that courage is always shaped by the society in which it must be exercised.

📝 Draft Question 1
Write your comparison paragraph following the four-step structure above. Use at least two transition words (however, although, while, in contrast, both... and, therefore, consequently). Underline each transition.
📓
📝 Draft Question 2
Write your closing paragraph. It should: restate your comparison claim in different words, explain what this comparison reveals about the theme beyond the novels, and end with a sentence about why these texts matter as a pair.
📓
📝 Draft Question 3 — Self-Assessment
Read your full draft (opening + body 1 + body 2 + comparison + closing). In 3–4 sentences: What is the strongest part of your essay? What is the weakest part — and what would make it stronger?
