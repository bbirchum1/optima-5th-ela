How I Became a Ghost
🎙️ Week 24 · Fluency

This week you opened How I Became a Ghost by Tim Tingle. Isaac introduces himself in a way no narrator has before — as a ghost who isn’t a ghost yet. Today you’ll practice reading the opening passage with accuracy, smoothness, and the weight it deserves.

Pushmataha appeared before us — not as the broken man who had signed the treaties, but as he had been in his time of power: tall, certain, unafraid. He looked at each of us in turn. The Choctaw Four. Four young people who had survived what was supposed to destroy us.

'Remember us,' he said. Not as a plea. As a charge. 'Remember what we lost. Remember what we kept. And tell the story truly — every part of it, the fire and the ice and the bloody footprints and the laughter and the ghost walkers and the children who learned to be strong because they had no other choice.'

MAYBE YOU HAVE never read a book written by a ghost before. I am a ghost. I am not a ghost when this book begins, so you have to pay very close attention. I should tell you something else. I see things before they happen. You are probably thinking, “I wish I could see things before they happen.”

Be careful what you wish for.
— Tim Tingle, How I Became a Ghost, Chapter 1

🗺️ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video above. Listen to how your teacher reads Isaac’s opening words — the weight in “I am a ghost,” the pause before “Be careful what you wish for.”
2
Read for Accuracy
Read the passage aloud slowly. Say every word correctly. Pay attention to the all-caps opening — that’s not a mistake. Tim Tingle wants those words to land.
3
Read for Smoothness
Read again. Keep your voice flowing. Let the sentences move naturally. The short last sentence — “Be careful what you wish for” — should feel like a quiet warning, not rushed.
4
Read for Expression
Read one more time. Now bring Isaac to life. He is speaking from the future — he already knows what happened. Let your voice carry that weight. Slow down on “Be careful what you wish for.”
5
Record and Submit
When you feel ready, record yourself reading the passage with accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Upload your recording to Canvas.
✅ Fluency Self-Check
🎯 Did I say each word correctly, including “Choctaw” and “Mississippi”?
🌊 Did my reading sound smooth — like a story being told, not a list of words?
🎭 Did my voice carry the feeling of Isaac’s warning? Did “Be careful what you wish for” land the way it should?
