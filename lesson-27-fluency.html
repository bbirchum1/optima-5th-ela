Make it land.

📜 Week 27 Fluency Passage — Chapter 8: Saved by a Whisker
Twenty minutes later the miner was seated on a nail keg in a corner of the hotel porch, and Praiseworthy was snipping away with the shears. Quartz Jackson insisted that every lock be caught as it fell. Jack was kept busy holding a washpan under Praiseworthy’s busy scissors.

Quartz Jackson’s face began to appear, snip by snip, like a statue being chipped out of stone. When Praiseworthy had finished, the miner turned to look at himself in the hotel window pane, and he almost jumped out of his jackboots.

“By the Great Horn Spoon!” he said. “Is that me?”

Quartz Jackson was obviously pleased. “Why, I’d forgot I was so young!”
— Sid Fleischman, By the Great Horn Spoon!, Chapter 8

⚓ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video. Notice how your teacher slows at “like a statue being chipped out of stone.” That simile deserves a pause. Then listen for the beat before “By the Great Horn Spoon!” — the novel’s whole title as a punchline.
2
Read for Accuracy
Read slowly. Focus on jackboots, shears, obviously, and Praiseworthy. If you stumble, return to the start of that sentence.
3
Read for Smoothness
Read again at a comfortable pace. Let “snip by snip, like a statue being chipped out of stone” flow as one phrase. Don’t chop up the simile.
4
Read for Expression
Praiseworthy is calm and precise — he’s a professional. Quartz Jackson is genuinely astonished. Let “By the Great Horn Spoon!” be a real exclamation of surprise, followed by “Why, I’d forgot I was so young!” with the wonder of a man seeing himself clearly for the first time in months.
5
Record and Submit
Record with full accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Upload to Canvas.
