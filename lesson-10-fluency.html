📜 Week 10 Fluency Passage
Nat worked at his bookkeeping until the chandlery grew dark around him, and then he lit a candle and went on working. When the chandlery was closed and everyone else was gone, he opened his books. He had borrowed them from Dr. Prince’s library — Latin grammar, French vocabulary, a treatise on mathematics. He read until his eyes were too tired to read anymore. Then he put his head down and slept for a few hours. Then he rose and read again.
— Jean Lee Latham, Carry On, Mr. Bowditch, Chapter 6 (adapted for fluency)

🗺️ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video above. Notice how your teacher paces the passage — steady and unhurried, like Nat himself. Listen for the pause at “and then he lit a candle.” That pause matters.
2
Read for Accuracy
Read aloud slowly. Focus on every word — especially chandlery, treatise, mathematics, vocabulary. Use your syllable knowledge to decode any word you stumble on.
3
Read for Smoothness
Read again without stopping. Let the sentences flow. There are long sentences here — keep your breath steady. The rhythm of this passage should feel like steady, patient work.
4
Read for Expression
Read one final time with expression. This is Nat at his most determined. Your voice should carry that quiet, unstoppable quality. Slow down on “Then he rose and read again” — let those words land.
5
Record and Submit
Record yourself reading the passage with accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Upload your recording to Canvas.
✅ Fluency Self-Check
🎯Did I read every word correctly, including chandlery and treatise?
🌊Did my reading sound smooth — patient and flowing, not rushed?
🎭Did my voice show Nat’s quiet determination — especially at “Then he rose and read again”?
