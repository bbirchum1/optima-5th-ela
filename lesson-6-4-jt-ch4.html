Sentence 2 — Fix It

This sentence has mistakes. Rewrite it correctly in your notebook, using the model as a guide.

rab a quiet apprentice at the boston observer helped johnny prove his innocence at the trial

Corrected:

Rab, a quiet apprentice at the Boston Observer, helped Johnny prove his innocence at the trial.

Compare with the model: “Rab” starts the sentence—capital. “Boston Observer” is a proper name—both words capitalized. “A quiet apprentice at the Boston Observer” is an appositive renaming Rab—commas before and after. “Johnny” is a proper name—capital. Add a period at the end.

Morphology · Explain & Justify
Roots Across the Novel: Chapters 1–4
🌱
Teacher connects dict and scrib/script roots to words from across Chapters 1–4

Two Latin roots appear throughout academic and literary vocabulary. Dict means “say” or “declare.” Scrib and script mean “write.” Knowing these roots helps you decode unfamiliar words.

📌 Roots Across the Story

describe (de + scrib) — Johnny had to describe his bad hand to every master in Boston

verdict (ver + dict) — every shop seemed to deliver the same verdict: you won’t do

predict (pre + dict) — no one could predict that Johnny would remember the Lyte cup

manuscript (manu + script) — at the Observer, Rab handled manuscripts and printed broadsides

prescription (pre + script) — the unspoken prescription for his despair was to go to Merchant Lyte

Match each word to its meaning. Tap a word, then tap its definition.

dictate
to say what will happen before it does
inscribe
to say aloud for someone to write down
manuscript
to write an order or direction beforehand
predict
to write or carve into a surface
prescribe
a document written by hand

The Rising Eye

Mr. Hancock commissions a silver sugar basin from the Lapham shop—the biggest order they’ve had in years. Johnny takes charge of the work, pushing Dove and Dusty hard. He visits Paul Revere, who critiques his design and offers to buy his time. Mr. Lapham warns Johnny about his pride. His career as a silversmith is over.

Argument Writing — Day 4: Putting It Together
✓ What Is a Claim? → ✓ Reasons → ✓ Evidence → ★ Putting It Together → ○ Draft → ○ Revise & Publish

🎯 Today’s focus: Put it all together—a claim, two reasons, and evidence for each. This is the full shape of an argument.

👀 Watch How — Alex

Alex now has all the pieces and puts them into an Argument Map. Watch how the claim, reasons, and evidence connect.

📌 Alex’s Claim

Johnny Tremain is changed by his suffering—not destroyed by it—because he learns that he needs other people.

Reason 1

Because Rab helps him when no one else does.

Evidence 1

In Chapter 4, Rab arrives at the jail with food and blankets, and finds a lawyer to defend Johnny at trial.

Reason 2

Because Cilla and Isannah’s loyalty saves him.

Evidence 2

Cilla testifies at trial about seeing the cup in July, which proves Johnny owned it before the alleged theft.

Notice: Each reason answers “why” the claim is true. Each piece of evidence names something specific that actually happened.
