🌱 Morphology: Suffixes -ive / -ative
Turning verbs and nouns into adjectives
🌱
Morphology · Practice
Using -ive / -ative Words
🌱
Teacher reviews -ive / -ative suffixes

The suffixes -ive and -ative turn verbs or nouns into adjectives — words that describe. Today, use these words in sentences connected to Chapters 11–12.

📌 Quick Reminder

create (verb) + -ive = creative (adjective) — having the ability to create

protect (verb) + -ive = protective (adjective) — tending to protect

imagine (verb) + -ative = imaginative (adjective) — full of imagination

talk (verb) + -ative = talkative (adjective) — inclined to talk a lot

🧩

Teacher models dialogue punctuation from Chapters 11–12

🔠 Spelling: Schwa & Reduced Vowels
Apply — Identify and spell the schwa sound
🔠
Spelling · Day 3
Applying Schwa Patterns
🔠
Teacher reviews schwa sounds in unstressed syllables

The schwa sound is the lazy “uh” in an unstressed syllable. It can be spelled with any vowel — that’s what makes it tricky. Today, find the schwa and spell the words correctly.

📌 Quick Reminder

separate — the schwa is the second “a” (sounds like “sep-uh-rate”)

different — the schwa is the “e” (sounds like “diff-uh-rent”)

comfort — the schwa is the “o” (sounds like “cumf-urt”)

🎮

Practice: Sort these words by which vowel makes the schwa sound. Tap a word, then tap the column.

separate different calendar excellent grammar medicine
Schwa spelled with A
Schwa spelled with E or I
🪶 Show What You Know · Spelling
Circle the syllable with the schwa sound in each word, then spell the word correctly: sep_rate, diff_rent, comf_rt
Write each word with the correct spelling. Name the vowel that makes the schwa sound in each one.

RWM — Read like a Reader, Read like a Writer, Mimic
“Kit had no idea what happened to her thin plain features when she smiled.”
📖 Read like a Reader
What is the narrator telling us about Kit that Kit doesn’t know about herself? Think about what this reveals — Kit sees Prudence one way, but the narrator sees something Kit is blind to.
✏️ Read like a Writer
How does Speare use this sentence to show us something about Kit that Kit can’t see? Why is this more powerful than just saying “Kit had a beautiful smile”? Notice how the narrator pulls back to reveal something the character is unaware of.

What Good Readers Do
Track how setting reveals character

As you read today, track how Kit’s secret life at Hannah’s reveals a different side of her character. At home she’s restless and resentful. At Hannah’s she’s patient, generous, and creative. What does this contrast tell us about what Kit really needs?

🧠 As you read, ask yourself

What kind of person does Kit become at Hannah’s cottage — and why can’t she be that person in Wethersfield?

What risks is Kit taking by keeping this double life? What could go wrong?

Kit teaches Prudence; harvest season deepens

Kit begins bringing Prudence to Hannah’s cottage to teach her to read. Prudence blossoms under Kit’s attention. Meanwhile, harvest intensifies and Kit’s double life grows harder to sustain. Watch for the moments when Kit feels most free — and the moments when she feels most trapped.
