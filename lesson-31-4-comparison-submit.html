What does a strong peer reviewer do — and what do you do with the feedback?

A peer reviewer is not a proofreader. They read to understand — and then ask: Is the claim clear? Is the evidence convincing? Does the comparison actually show a meaningful difference? Today you review a classmate’s work (or self-review) and make targeted revisions.

Sentence 1 — The Correct Model

The most powerful comparison essays do not simply describe — they argue why the difference between two texts matters.

Sentence 2 — Edit It

both novels demonstrate courage however the contexts are so different that they reveal entirely different things about what it means to be brave on the one hand kit must hide her compassion and on the other hand praiseworthy must display his competence in a world that will respect it

Corrected: Both novels demonstrate courage; however, the contexts are so different that they reveal entirely different things about what it means to be brave. On one hand, Kit must hide her compassion; on the other hand, Praiseworthy must display his competence in a world that will respect it.

📌 Grammar Spiral Review — All Year

Sentence types: compound (FANBOYS) · complex (subordinating conjunctions) · compound-complex

Openers: participial phrase · adverb · prepositional phrase · dependent clause

Punctuation: commas in compound, complex, and introductory clauses · dialogue · semicolon · em dash

Peer Review: Read to Understand, Then Respond
Specific feedback that helps — not just praise or nitpicking
📌 The Three Questions of Good Peer Review

1. What is the comparison claim? Can you state it in one sentence? If not — the writer needs to make it clearer.

2. Does the comparison paragraph show a meaningful difference? Not just “they’re different” — but WHY the difference matters for the theme.

3. Is the evidence specific? Does the writer name scenes, moments, or quotes — or just make general statements?

💡

How to write helpful feedback: Be specific and kind. Not: “Your essay is good.” Instead: “Your comparison claim is clear — I can see the difference between the two novels. Your Body Paragraph 1 has strong evidence. In the comparison paragraph, I couldn’t tell why the difference between the two novels MATTERS. One more sentence explaining that would make your argument much stronger.”

👥 Peer Review Question 1
Read the full draft (a classmate’s, or your own with fresh eyes). Write your answers to the three peer review questions: (1) State the comparison claim in your own words. (2) Is the comparison paragraph showing WHY the difference matters? (3) Are the evidence examples specific?
📓
👥 Peer Review Question 2
Write two pieces of specific feedback: (1) Name the strongest part of the essay and explain specifically why it works. (2) Name one part that could be stronger and give a specific suggestion for how to improve it.
📓
👥 Peer Review Question 3 — Week Synthesis
Looking back at the week: What did you learn about comparison writing that you didn’t know before? What was the hardest part — and what helped? In 3–4 sentences, reflect on your process from Plan (Day 1) to this final review.
