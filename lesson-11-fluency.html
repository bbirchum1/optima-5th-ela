This week Nat Bowditch sailed for the first time — nine years of self-education finally proved against the real sea. Today’s passage is from Chapter 10: the moment Nat signs on as supercargo and looks ahead. Read it as if something enormous has just become possible.

📜 Week 11 Fluency Passage
When the papers were signed, Nat walked out into the Salem morning. The harbor lay blue and wide before him. He could see the Henry at the wharf, her masts rising clean against the sky. For nine years he had looked at ships through windows and doorways. Now, for the first time, he would sail on one. He straightened his shoulders. He had the mathematics. He had the stars. What more did a man need?
— Jean Lee Latham, Carry On, Mr. Bowditch, Chapter 10 (adapted for fluency)

🗺️ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video above. Notice how your teacher paces the passage — especially the two final questions. “He had the mathematics. He had the stars.” These are not questions of doubt. They are statements of certainty. And then: “What more did a man need?”
2
Read for Accuracy
Read slowly and clearly. Every word must be crisp: harbor, straightened, mathematics. Use your syllable knowledge for mathematics: math-e-mat-ics.
3
Read for Smoothness
Read again at a steady, unhurried pace. Short sentences need space. “He had the mathematics.” Pause. “He had the stars.” Pause. Then: the question.
4
Read for Expression
This is a moment of quiet confidence — not excitement, not fanfare. Nat is certain. Let your voice carry that certainty. The final question should sound like an answer, not a doubt.
5
Record and Submit
Record yourself with accuracy, smoothness, and the quiet confidence that matches Nat’s voice in this moment. Upload to Canvas.
✅ Fluency Self-Check
🎯Did I read every word correctly, including mathematics?
🌊Did the short sentences get enough space — did I pause between them?
🎭Did my voice carry quiet certainty — especially in the final two sentences?
