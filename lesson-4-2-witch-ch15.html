Daily Oral Language
Study the model, then fix the sentence

Sentence 1 — The Correct Model

Tap words you notice are correct. Watch for capitals, commas, and punctuation.

William arrived at midnight and reported that the charter had vanished.

Sentence 2 — Fix It

This sentence has mistakes. Rewrite it correctly in your notebook, using the model as a guide.

william arrives at midnight yesterday and is reporting that the charter vanish from the table

Corrected:

William arrived at midnight yesterday and reported that the charter had vanished from the table.

Compare with the model: “arrives” should be “arrived” (past tense). “Is reporting” should be “reported” (consistent past tense). “Vanish” should be “had vanished” (past perfect). Capitalize “William.”

Sentence 3 — Fix It

governor andros arrive in hartford with seventy soldiers and the colonists refuses to hand over there charter

Corrected:

Governor Andros arrived in Hartford with seventy soldiers, and the colonists refused to hand over their charter.

Capitalize proper names (Governor Andros, Hartford). “Arrive” should be “arrived” and “refuses” should be “refused” (past tense consistency). “There” should be “their” (possessive). Add a comma before “and” joining two independent clauses.

Grammar · Introduction
Spotting Verb Tense Shifts
✏️
Teacher models verb tense consistency in narrative writing

When writing about events in a story, keep your verb tenses consistent. If you start in past tense, stay in past tense. Shifting between present and past confuses the reader.

👉 From the novel

Consistent: “The men gathered at Matthew’s house and argued about the charter.” — Both verbs are past tense.

Inconsistent: “The men gathered at Matthew’s house and argue about the charter.” — The shift from past to present is jarring.

Watch for: Past perfect (“had vanished”) is fine when an event happened before the main action.

🎯

Practice: Grammar Spotter! Each sentence has a verb tense problem. Tap the word that breaks consistency.

The angry men gathered at Matthew’s house and debates the future of the charter.

gathered debates future

Kit watched the soldiers march into Hartford and feels a thrill of recognition.

watched feels recognition

William knocked on the door and explains that he had changed his mind about the taxes.

knocked explains changed

The candles went dark and the charter disappears from the table.

went disappears table

Kit lay in bed that night and realizes she was proud of her uncle.

lay realizes proud

What is Matthew really saying here? This is not just about a document—it is about identity. What does “free men” mean to Matthew and the colonists?
✏️ Read like a Writer
Notice how Speare gives Matthew the strongest line in the chapter. He does not say “we want our charter back.” He says they will “show the world.” How does word choice turn a political statement into a theme statement about freedom?
