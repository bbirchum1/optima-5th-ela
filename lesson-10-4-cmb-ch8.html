Daily Oral Language
Model and fix — find all errors

Sentence 1 — The Correct Model

For nine years, Nathaniel Bowditch worked as a bookkeeper at the ship chandlery and taught himself mathematics by night.

Sentence 2 — Fix It

nathaniel bowditch works as a bookkeeper at ropes and hodges and he teaches the sailors how to use logarithms

Corrected: Nathaniel Bowditch worked as a bookkeeper at Ropes and Hodges and taught the sailors how to use logarithms.

Fixes: “Nathaniel Bowditch” (proper noun); “Ropes and Hodges” (proper noun — company name); “works” → “worked” and “teaches” → “taught” (tense shift corrected to consistent past).

Poetry Corner
A poem that echoes Mother’s words

In Chapter 4, Nat’s mother tells him her saying: “I will lift up my eyes unto the stars.” Those words come from one of the oldest poems in the world — Psalm 121, written thousands of years ago. Nat’s mother found comfort in them. As you finish Week 10, read them again.

📜 Week Closing Poem
from Psalm 121
King James Bible, 1611 — public domain
I will lift up mine eyes unto the hills,
from whence cometh my help.
My help cometh from the Lord,
which made heaven and earth.

He will not suffer thy foot to be moved:
he that keepeth thee will not slumber.

Look back across the whole week’s reading before reading the final chapter

Before you open Chapter 8, take a moment to look back at the full arc of Chapters 5–8. A week of reading is like a wave: something builds, reaches a peak, and changes. What has changed for Nat?

🧠 The Week’s Arc

At the start of Chapter 5, Nat’s whistle dies on the street. By Chapter 7, he is finding errors that no one else sees. What has changed — in Nat’s circumstances? In his mindset? In what Latham is showing us about who he is?

As you read Chapter 8, pay attention to how Nat relates to the sailors he works with. What does he begin to do that no one asked him to do?

Alex assembles all pieces into the final plan: introduction box, two body boxes, conclusion box, sources box.

BEFORE
Alex’s rough notes: Hook = fog scene. Body 1 = chandlery nights + notebooks. Body 2 = Moore’s errors + teaching sailors.
AFTER
Alex’s complete 5-box plan: (1) INTRO: Hook: December fog, ship navigating blind → Nat Bowditch, Salem mathematician → Claim. (2) BODY 1: Subtopic: self-made scholar. Evidence A: chandlery nights (Chs. 5–8). Evidence B: fills notebooks. (3) BODY 2: Subtopic: knowledge for everyone. Evidence A: Moore’s corrections (Ch. 13). Evidence B: teaches sailors (Chs. 11, 16). (4) CONCLUSION: Return to fog → synthesize both subtopics → why it matters. (5) SOURCES: Latham, Jean Lee.

Notice: The plan names specific chapters for each piece of evidence. This makes drafting much easier — Alex knows exactly where to look.
