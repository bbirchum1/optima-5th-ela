📜 Week 28 Fluency Passage — Chapter 12: Bullwhip
When they reached the Empire Hotel the porch loungers gazed at them in a kind of awe. A mutter of voices arose.

“Knocked that outlaw seventeen feet.”
“Up hill.”
“Nineteen feet — the way it was told to me.”
“Nineteen feet and eleven inches. They measured it.”

Praiseworthy stopped in the doorway. He looked at Jack, who broke into a muddy smile. Then the butler turned, peering at the whiskered faces grinning in the yellow light from the hotel.

“Gents,” he said, “from where I was standing — it looked twenty-three feet at least.”

A miner swallowed his cud of tobacco. “O be joyful.”

“Come along, Jamoka Jack,” said Praiseworthy, turning into the hotel.
— Sid Fleischman, By the Great Horn Spoon!, Chapter 12

⛏️ Your Fluency Path
1
Listen
Watch the video. Notice how your teacher reads the escalating numbers: “seventeen feet” → “up hill” (pause) → “nineteen feet” → “nineteen feet and eleven inches. They measured it.” Each line tops the last. Then comes the turn: Praiseworthy’s “twenty-three feet at least” — calm, dry, and perfect.
2
Read for Accuracy
Focus on: peering, whiskered, Praiseworthy, and O be joyful (an old expression of delight). If you stumble, return to the sentence start.
3
Read for Smoothness
The short dialogue lines need a natural pause between each — like different voices in a crowd. Don’t rush the ascending numbers.
4
Read for Expression
The miners are awed and proud of a stranger. Praiseworthy is amused and slightly reluctant — but he decides to play along. “Twenty-three feet at least” should be deadpan-perfect. “Come along, Jamoka Jack” is warm and private — just for Jack.
5
Record and Submit
Record with full accuracy, smoothness, and expression. Upload to Canvas.
